Why EEG was a Game-Changer

While image reconstruction was possible with fMRI, using EEG was a major leap forward, overcoming skepticism about the technology's fidelity.

Superior Temporal Resolution

EEG measures activity in milliseconds, allowing researchers to track the rapid formation of a mental image (est. 170ms).

Practicality & Accessibility

EEG systems are portable, affordable, and widely available compared to massive, expensive fMRI scanners, opening doors for real-world applications.

Public Profile & Ethical Questions

The work captured global media attention, often with "mind-reading" headlines. This sparked vital conversations about potential applications and ethics.

Potential Applications

Ideas included communication aids for non-verbal individuals and tools for law enforcement to visualize eyewitness memories.

Cognitive Liberty

Bioethicists raised concerns about the right to freedom of thought if internal mental states were no longer private, a debate Dr. Nemrodov himself encouraged.

The Next Frontier: Generative Neuromodulation

At Dandelion Science, Dr. Nemrodov's work has evolved from reading brain signals to actively guiding them. This section highlights a flagship project developing a novel biomarker for Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD), showcasing the power of this new therapeutic paradigm.

A New Biomarker for AMD

The team developed a method using frequency-tagged videos to precisely measure how the brain processes different types of visual information (e.g., fine details vs. coarse outlines). This is measured using Steady-State Visual Evoked Potentials (SSVEP).

The key finding was that the ratio of the brain's response to low-frequency (coarse) vs. high-frequency (detailed) information serves as a powerful, objective biomarker of AMD severity.

The chart to the right visualizes this core finding, showing the significantly different neural response patterns between healthy individuals and AMD patients. This demonstrates a clear, measurable brain signal that correlates with the disease.
